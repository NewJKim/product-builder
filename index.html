Fast Food Calorie Counter

Keeping track of what you eat at fast food restaurants is one of the simplest ways to stay on top of your daily calorie intake. Meals from popular chains can range from under 300 calories to well over 1,500 — and it adds up fast.

This free tool lets you select items from McDonald's, Panda Express, Subway, and Chipotle — or enter any custom item — and instantly see your running daily total against a personal goal. No account, no app, no signup required.

How to Use This Tool

Three steps to track a meal — takes about ten seconds.

  1. 1Choose a restaurant from the dropdown and select the menu item you ordered. It will be added to your daily log with its calorie count.
  2. 2Use the Custom Item row to log anything not in the menu list — just type the name and calorie count.
  3. 3Watch your total and remaining calories update in real time. Adjust the Daily Goal to match your personal target, then tap Clear All to reset at the end of the day.

Why Calorie Tracking Works

Studies consistently show that simply being aware of calorie intake — without any strict diet — leads to better food choices over time. Fast food is easy to underestimate: a single combo meal can account for more than half a standard 2,000-calorie daily goal. By logging each item, including sides and drinks, you get an accurate picture of where you stand.

The daily recommended calorie intake varies by age, sex, and activity level. A common reference point is 2,000 calories per day for adults, but your personal goal may differ. Consult a registered dietitian or healthcare provider for personalized guidance.
